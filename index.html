A Visionary Surgeon

Dr. Mani Menon stands as one of the most influential figures in modern urological surgery. His pioneering work in robotic-assisted surgery has revolutionized the treatment of prostate cancer and transformed the lives of countless patients worldwide.

As a surgeon, researcher, and educator, Dr. Menon has dedicated his career to advancing minimally invasive surgical techniques, with a particular focus on preserving quality of life for cancer patients. His innovations have set new standards in precision, recovery time, and patient outcomes.

Career Milestones

1982

Completed residency in Urology at Johns Hopkins Hospital

1990s

Joined Henry Ford Health System, beginning his journey in advanced urological procedures

2000

Performed first robotic-assisted prostatectomy at Henry Ford Hospital

2010

Founded the Vattikuti Urology Institute, establishing a world-class center for robotic surgery

2015

Moved to AdventHealth to continue advancing robotic surgical techniques

Present

Continues to innovate and mentor the next generation of robotic surgeons

Revolutionary Achievements
